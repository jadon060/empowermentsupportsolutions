How Hayden Earns Points

This system is designed to reward consistent effort over time, not quick wins. Points are earned by showing maturity, responsibility, respect, school effort, and active progress in ILS.

College & School Responsibility
+1 Point — Prepared for School Shows up ready for class with needed materials, a charged device if needed, and a cooperative attitude.
+2 Points — Attends Class as Expected Follows his school schedule, attends class, and avoids unnecessary absences or skipping.
+2 Points — Completes Homework or Class Assignments Works on homework, turns in assignments, studies, or completes required class tasks.
+3 Points — Strong School Follow-Through Stays consistent with school responsibilities, asks for help when needed, and shows improvement with college expectations.
Respect & Personal Responsibility
+1 Point — Respectful Communication Speaks respectfully, listens when others are talking, and avoids rude or dismissive responses.
+2 Points — Accepts Redirection Calmly Responds appropriately when corrected, reminded, or redirected by family, staff, or school personnel.
+2 Points — Handles Frustration Appropriately Uses calm words, takes a break, asks for help, or redirects instead of arguing or shutting down.
+3 Points — Shows Maturity in a Difficult Moment Makes a positive choice during a frustrating situation and avoids escalating the problem.
Household Work & Taking Initiative
+1 Point — Completes a Basic Chore Completes a simple household task such as taking out trash, cleaning up after himself, or putting items away.
+2 Points — Does a Chore Without Being Asked Multiple Times Follows through after a reminder or completes the task without repeated prompting.
+2 Points — Takes Initiative Around the House Notices something that needs to be done and helps with it, such as wiping a counter, picking up trash, organizing an area, or helping with laundry.
+3 Points — Shows Consistent Household Responsibility Completes chores, keeps his area cleaner, and shows that he is building stronger independent living habits at home.
ILS Progress
+1 Point — Showing Up Ready Arrives on time, participates, and starts session with a cooperative attitude.
+2 Points — Active Participation in ILS Stays engaged in teaching, answers questions, and puts in visible effort.
+2 Points — Completing an ILS Task Finishes an assigned activity such as budgeting, planning, communication practice, school planning, household responsibility, or community skill work.
+3 Points — Strong Follow-Through Applies the skill outside of session, remembers expectations, or demonstrates improved decision-making.

Important Point Policy

Family and ESS staff have full control to add, deny, adjust, or remove points based on Hayden’s performance, behavior, follow-through, honesty, respect, school effort, household responsibility, safety, and overall participation. Points are earned, not handed out freely.

Reward Menu

Rewards are meant to motivate long-term consistency. Larger rewards require sustained effort over time.

40 Points Small Preferred Reward A simple approved incentive such as a snack, drink, small item, or another basic preferred reward.

120 Points $100 Gift Card Major reward earned only through steady, respectful, responsible, and consistent progress over time.
