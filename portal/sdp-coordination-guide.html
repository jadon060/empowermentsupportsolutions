SDP Coordination Guide

Public overview of how Empowerment Support Solutions (ESS) coordinates services within California’s Self-Determination Program (SDP). Designed for participants, families, Service Coordinators, and Financial Management Services (FMS).

Purpose-Driven Family-Centered FMS-Ready

How ESS Fits in SDP

ESS operates as a non-vendored provider within SDP. The participant and circle of support select ESS, and payment flows through the participant’s FMS. Regional Center SCs support planning and authorization.
  • Participant & Family: Lead the plan and choose services.
  • Service Coordinator (SC): Aligns IPP goals, documents authorizations.
  • FMS: Manages budget, agreements, and provider payments.
  • ESS: Provides staffing and skills support aligned to the PCP/IPP, with clear documentation for each hour of service.

Meeting Objectives

  • Review current IPP goals and any Person-Centered Plan notes relevant to services.
  • Confirm authorized hours and applicable budget categories.
  • Identify the FMS and model (Bill-Payer, Co-Employer, or Sole-Employer).
  • Confirm orientation/training requirements and any additional documentation.
  • Record responsible parties and target dates for each next step.

Coordination Timeline

1) Plan Alignment
  • Match IPP goals to ESS support areas (life skills, community integration, health & safety, behavioral support, etc.).
  • Note goals to begin now and goals to phase in later.
2) Authorized Hours & Budget
  • Document monthly hours per goal or service area.
  • Capture scheduling constraints, service caps, and tentative start date.
3) FMS Coordination
  • Record FMS name, model, and onboarding contact.
  • List required FMS documents (e.g., W-9, agreement, insurance if applicable).
4) Orientation & Compliance
  • List training/orientation links and proof-of-completion instructions.
  • Note any additional case-specific requirements (e.g., background checks, CPR).
5) Authorization & Start
  • Document who submits what (ESS, SC, FMS) and the expected processing time.
  • Confirm start date, documentation cadence, and monthly invoicing window.

Information Checklist

  • IPP goals and relevant PCP notes
  • Authorized hours and budget category
  • FMS contact and model
  • Orientation/training links and requirements
  • Required documents list and due dates

Documents Provided by ESS

  • W-9 and service agreement (to FMS)
  • Staffing plan aligned to IPP goals
  • Timesheet and daily note templates
  • Projected start date and schedule
